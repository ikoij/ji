FAQ


How does swapping on Tezoswap work?

Alice and Bob want to swap NFTs with each other. Alice selects the NFTs in her wallet she wants to swap. She enters Bob’s wallet address and proposes the swap. Bob sees the content of Alice’s NFTs and adds his own NFTs. He initiates the swap. Alice now sees the swap content and she can either reject or accept the swap, but she can’t change anything anymore.

What happens with my NFTs during the swap?

Your NFTs will be transferred into the escrow contract for the duration of the swap. They’ll be kept safe there until the swap is completed. Neither we nor others have access to them. You can cancel the swap and get them back as long as they are in escrow. A cancellation will transfer your NFTs back into your wallet.

Why do I need to approve my NFTs?

We need to be able to transfer tokens on your behalf into the Tezoswap escrow to execute the swap. Our smart contract can’t change your token’s behaviour or do something else with the token. You can always revoke the approval through services like etherscan or revoke.cash. We actually recommend that for all your NFTs. Disconnecting your wallet from a website is not enough. Stay safe.

Do both parties have to propose a swap?

No. One party proposes the swap to the other. The counterpart then just goes to open swaps on Tezoswap and continues the process.

Which tokens are supported?

We are supporting ERC-721, ERC-1155 and Ether to top up the swap. We are exploring options to add more tokens on a later stage. Feel free to reach out to share tokens you would like to add to a swap.
